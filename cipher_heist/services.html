Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our services

Vulnerability scanning is an automated process that identifies known vulnerabilities in systems and applications. Penetration testing goes further by simulating real-world attacks to exploit vulnerabilities, demonstrating their potential impact, and testing defensive mechanisms. Our penetration tests combine both automated tools and manual testing by security experts.

We recommend quarterly vulnerability scanning for most organizations, with annual penetration testing at minimum. High-risk industries or organizations handling sensitive data should consider more frequent testing (semi-annual or quarterly). Additional assessments should be conducted after major system changes, new application deployments, or security incidents.

Yes, all our assessment reports include detailed remediation guidance. For Professional and Enterprise packages, we offer consultation calls to help your team understand and implement fixes. We can also provide retesting services to verify that vulnerabilities have been properly addressed.

Our methodologies align with recognized standards including OWASP Testing Guide, NIST SP 800-115, PTES, and ISO 27001. We can tailor assessments to meet specific compliance requirements like PCI DSS, HIPAA, GDPR, or SOC 2. Our reports include mapping to compliance frameworks when requested.

Our comprehensive reports include: executive summary for management, detailed technical findings with risk ratings, evidence of vulnerabilities (screenshots, code snippets), step-by-step reproduction instructions, and prioritized remediation recommendations. Enterprise clients receive additional deliverables like raw tool outputs and retesting results.
